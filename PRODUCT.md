# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Agent Platform is for builders designing long-running human-agent collaboration systems. The primary users are engineers, product teams, and technical evaluators who need to understand how projects, agents, tools, skills, channels, schedules, artifacts, threads, runs, resources, and workspace views fit together.

The flagship user-facing surface is the workspace UI: a focused workbench where humans and agents review state, inspect artifacts, discuss next steps, and trigger actions without losing the history of work.

## Product Purpose

The product demonstrates and implements a package-first, project-centric agent platform. Success means a contributor can understand the platform model quickly, load a project from filesystem metadata, and see a polished workspace composed from reusable registry components instead of one-off pages.

The hiring project is the default story because it makes the runtime concrete: candidate review, assistant analysis, supporting sources, agents, approvals, and follow-up actions all happen over time.

## Brand Personality

Calm, capable, and precise.

The interface should feel like serious collaboration software: clear enough for operational work, polished enough to sell the concept, and restrained enough that the metadata-driven model remains the star.

## Anti-references

Do not make the product look like a generic AI dashboard, a pile of rounded cards, or a demo page with decorative panels. Avoid nested boxes, oversized typography, repeated eyebrow labels, ambiguous pills, and component chrome that makes every block compete for attention.

Do not hard-code view-specific business logic or style a single workspace as a bespoke page. The UI should prove the registry and metadata composition model.

## Design Principles

1. Metadata should feel powerful: views are composed from clear, reusable components.
2. Work should feel continuous: queue, assistant, artifact, sources, agents, and actions belong to one connected workspace.
3. Hierarchy should do the explaining: important work is prominent; supporting chrome stays quiet.
4. Components should be understandable: aliases, names, and visual affordances should be generic, reusable, and obvious.
5. Polish should support trust: screenshots and docs should look credible enough to represent the platform.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Aim for WCAG AA contrast for text and controls. Preserve keyboard-readable structure, distinct focus states, reduced-motion safety, and visual affordances that do not rely on color alone.
